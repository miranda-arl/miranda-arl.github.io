Project Objectives/Outcomes: • At the end of the project, we want to get a better sense of the amount of police misconduct in the state of California • The goal is to analyze all recorded data about past police misconduct records • Our data will help fuel the topic for the article that our investigative journalist team will publish Project Overview • The Investigative Reporting Program, in collaboration with Stanford, a consortium of California's largest news organizations, and the Berkeley Institute of Data Science is building and analyzing a first-of-its-kind dataset of police misconduct at every law enforcement agency in California. Our main tool is a new state transparency law, SB 1421, that for the first time makes public internal affairs reports and other records of police officers accused of dishonesty, sexual assault or involved in use of force incidents. With this dataset, this collaboration hopes to produce groundbreaking investigative journalism that sheds new light on years of police misconduct throughout the entire state.
